Extended Matrix is no longer "just" the documentation method developed at
CNR-ISPC. It is now an active building block of two converging European
initiatives that are reshaping how cultural heritage knowledge is produced,
shared and preserved.

## StratiGraph

The **StratiGraph** project — funded by the European Commission — picks
up the EM stratigraphic-graph framework and scales it to the level of a
shared European infrastructure. Within StratiGraph, EM provides the
typed vocabulary and the reasoning model; the project, in turn, brings
the resources to harden EM as a long-term standard: versioning policy,
**LTS** (long-term support) releases, formal change-control, and
sustained funding for the maintenance of the formal language.

For users, this means the EM you adopt today will still be readable,
documented and tooled in ten years.

## ECCCH and CIDOC-CRM alignment

StratiGraph is also where EM's **CIDOC-CRM alignment** matures. The
property graph generated by `s3dgraphy` already maps to CIDOC-CRM
classes; the StratiGraph work programme commits to publishing the full
mapping spec, the validation suite, and a reference triplestore export.
This makes EM-produced data first-class citizens of the
**European Collaborative Cloud for Cultural Heritage (ECCCH)** — the
Commission's flagship infrastructure for FAIR, interoperable heritage
data across the continent.

In practical terms, an EM graph authored on a single excavation can
travel — without translation — into the same federated knowledge space
as a national digital library, a museum collection, or a monument
register.

## What changes for the community

- A clear **standardisation roadmap**: LTS policy, semantic-version
  governance, and a published change-control process.
- A **published CIDOC-CRM mapping** maintained as part of the official
  EM specification, not as a side project.
- **Funded ECCCH connectors**: out-of-the-box bridges from EM Tools and
  Heriverse to the Collaborative Cloud's data spaces.
- Continued integration with national infrastructures (PNRR / H2IOSC in
  Italy) and with the EM Framework's own tools.

For day-to-day users this is mostly transparent — graphs and projects
already authored remain valid, and the new export targets become
available from EM Tools and Heriverse without extra work.
