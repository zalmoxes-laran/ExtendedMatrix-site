After the release of **EM 1.5 LTS** and the relaunch of
[extendedmatrix.org](https://extendedmatrix.org), the recurring
meetings calendar is reopening. Two appointments in June, one for the
broader community and one more technical — both public, both on
Microsoft Teams, both reachable without a Microsoft account.

## 🍻 EM Hour — Wednesday 11 June, 16:00–17:00 CET

The first **community happy hour** of the new cycle. Casual format: no
slides, no recording. On the table:

- What shipped in **EM 1.5 LTS** — Landscape mode, CronoFilter,
  Document Manager, Proxy Box Creator, and `s3dgraphy` standalone on
  PyPI.
- The new site at extendedmatrix.org and the
  [dev tracker](https://dev.extendedmatrix.org/) for the 1.6 line.
- The state of **StratiGraph** (ECCCH project) and where the formal
  language is heading next.
- Open floor: bring questions, demos, screen-shares.

👉 **Join:**
[Teams meeting](https://teams.microsoft.com/meet/391652615209179?p=zvw8dplEO88EhblzOS)
· Event page on the site: [extendedmatrix.org/events/2026-06-11-june](https://extendedmatrix.org/events/2026-06-11-june/)

## 🛠️ EM Developer Meeting — Tuesday 23 June, 15:00–16:00 CET

Public coordination for people working on the codebase — EM Tools,
s3dgraphy, pyArchInit-EM bridge, the manual, integrations. Notes-first
format, modelled on the
[blender.org module meetings](https://devtalk.blender.org/): open by
default, decisions visible. Standing agenda:

1. Announcements & releases since last meeting.
2. PRs and proposals on the table.
3. Architectural decisions to discuss.
4. Open floor.

If you're thinking about contributing and don't know where to start,
this is the place to ask — bring the kind of work you'd like to do and
we'll match it to what's open.

👉 **Join:**
[Teams meeting](https://teams.microsoft.com/meet/388419352314569?p=qvWgByiUm0jGtpW4mQ)
· Event page in the Community section: [extendedmatrix.org/community](https://extendedmatrix.org/community/#dev-meetings)

---

See you in June. If you can't make it live, the EM Hour goes loosely
into our YouTube workflow when there's something worth keeping, and
the Dev Meeting decisions are recorded on the event page in plain
text — no recording needed for that one to be useful.
